August 2, 2026 is not a soft deadline. On that date, the EU AI Act's Annex III provisions become binding for enterprises deploying AI systems in the EU, including US-headquartered companies whose AI systems affect EU persons.

## What Annex III actually covers

Annex III lists the categories of AI systems that qualify as "high-risk" under the Act. If your AI system falls into one of these categories, you're subject to the full set of obligations in Title III, Chapter 2.

The relevant categories for enterprise AI agent deployments:

- **Critical infrastructure**: AI systems used as safety components of critical infrastructure
- **Education and vocational training**: AI determining access to education or professional opportunities
- **Employment and workers management**: AI for recruitment, performance evaluation, promotion decisions
- **Essential private and public services**: credit scoring, insurance risk assessment
- **Law enforcement**: AI used by law enforcement for risk assessments
- **Administration of justice**: AI assisting judicial authorities
- **Democratic processes**: AI used in elections

Most enterprise AI agents, including Thursdai deployments, are not automatically high-risk. The determination depends on your specific use case.

## The 12 obligations (summary)

For systems that are high-risk, the Act imposes 12 obligations: risk management system, data governance, technical documentation, record-keeping and logging, transparency, human oversight, accuracy and robustness, quality management, conformity assessment, registration, corrective actions and post-market monitoring.

The good news: most of these obligations map directly to engineering practices that good enterprise AI systems should have anyway. The audit trail, the human oversight controls, the documented risk management: these aren't new concepts. They're just now required.

## What to do before August 2, 2026

1. **Determine your risk category.** Have legal counsel assess whether your AI deployments fall under Annex III. This is not a technical decision. It requires legal interpretation.

2. **Document your governance controls.** If you're using Thursdai, your audit trail, policy enforcement and provenance documentation are largely built in. Download the FRIA and DPIA templates from /trust/annex-iii as a starting point.

3. **Complete your FRIA.** A Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment is required for deployers of high-risk AI systems before deployment. Start this now. It takes 4–8 weeks to complete properly.

4. **Verify your technical documentation.** Annex IV specifies what technical documentation must contain. Thursdai provides a pre-filled technical documentation template for Thursdai-based deployments at /trust/annex-iii.

5. **Register your system.** High-risk AI systems must be registered in the EU database before deployment. Allow 4–6 weeks for processing.
