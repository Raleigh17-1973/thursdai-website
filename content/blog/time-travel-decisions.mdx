In March 2025, your AI system recommended proceeding with a vendor contract. In August 2025, you discovered a GDPR issue with that vendor. A regulator asks: what did your AI know when it made that recommendation?

If your AI system is a chatbot, you have conversation logs. You don't have an answer.

## What gets recorded in a Thursdai snapshot

Every Thursdai inference records four things:

1. **The question**: verbatim, with the tenant context it was asked in
2. **The knowledge snapshot**: the exact version of the standard corpus and tenant corpus active at inference time
3. **The policy state**: the complete policy YAML set that governed the inference
4. **The role definitions**: which roles were configured, their scope and which corpus sections they could access

These four elements are stored as an immutable record. The Time-Travel feature is just a UI over that record. It lets you move a slider to any point in the past and reconstruct the exact answer that would have been given.

## Why this matters for regulated industries

Regulatory investigations are backward-looking. "What did your system know in March 2025?" is not a hypothetical. It's the actual form the question takes in an audit, in a dispute, in a post-incident review.

Most AI systems cannot answer it. They have conversation logs. They don't have knowledge versioning.

Thursdai records knowledge versions as a first-class architectural concern because regulated enterprises need to answer that question, not eventually but immediately, with a complete audit trail.

## How to use Time-Travel in practice

The Time-Travel scrubber on the home page shows a simple version: a slider with five snapshots of the same question answered at different points in history. In practice, the API exposes this as the `replay_at` parameter on `invoke_role()`.

Three use cases we see most often:

- **Regulatory audit**: reproduce a specific past recommendation with the exact knowledge active at the time
- **Incident investigation**: trace how an AI recommendation evolved as policies and knowledge changed
- **Model comparison**: run the same question with today's knowledge versus 12 months ago to see what changed and why
